The croissant is a buttery, flaky, and delicious pastry that originated in Austria but is now a staple of French bakeries and cafes. Known for its crescent shape, the croissant is made from a laminated dough
that involves folding butter into the dough multiple times to create thin layers. This process gives the croissant its signature airy texture and rich flavor, making it a popular choice for breakfast or a delightful
snack. Croissants can be enjoyed plain or filled with a variety of ingredients such as chocolate, almond paste, or ham and cheese, offering a versatile treat for any time of the day.
